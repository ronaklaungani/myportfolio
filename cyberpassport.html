Cyberpassport is an effort to:
○ Digitaly organize the standardized cybersecurity curriculam drawn by the National Institute of Standards & Technology (NIST), US Department of Commerce
○ Allow accredited organizations to organize workshops in compliance to the curriculam
○ Allow students & cybersecurity enthusiasts to find and book these workshops.

It is a research & product development project under Dr. Li-Chiou Chen, a professor at Pace University in New York City. The project is funded through a grant sponsored by the US Department of Defense - Cyber Scholarship Program.

My Role

○ Designed a mobile-first application by conducting research, producing interactive designs & intuitive customer experience
○ Crafted the style guidelines - color theory, iconography, typography, illustrations, imagery, graphic design & product documentation
○ Worked closely with project sponsor, researchers & developers to collaborate on product strategy, conception, vision & use-cases
○ Leveraged usability testing, feedback analysis & design critiques with internal stakeholders to identify customer insights.
